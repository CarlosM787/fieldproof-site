Questions contractors actually ask

Does the AI set my prices?

No — and it never will. The AI only turns your spoken walkthrough into a list of work items. Every price comes from your own cost book: your hourly rate, your material costs, your markup. Every line item shows exactly where its number came from.

What if it mishears me?

You see everything it heard before anything is priced, on one review screen. Fix a quantity, delete a line, add what's missing — takes seconds. And if you didn't say a measurement, it stays blank until you fill it in. It never guesses.

Does it work with no signal?

Recording works completely offline — basements, rural jobs, dead zones. Your walkthrough uploads and processes the moment you're back on signal, and your drafts are always saved on your phone.

How does my customer approve?

You text them a link. It opens in their browser — no app to install, no account to create. They see a clean, phase-by-phase proposal and tap Approve. You get the approval recorded with a timestamp.

What trades does it support?

Handyman, painting, drywall, and general work today — with pre-loaded price templates for each that you customize once and reuse forever. More trades are coming based on what early users ask for.

How is this different from Jobber or Joist?

Those are big service-business platforms — scheduling, dispatch, CRM, marketing — and they're priced like it, with key features like digital approval locked behind $200+/month tiers and free plans capped at a handful of documents. TalkEstimate does one thing extremely well: the fastest path from standing on a jobsite to an approved estimate. One flow, one flat price, nothing gated, nothing capped.

What about mid-job changes?

The customer adds work halfway through? Hit "+ Change" on the job, say what changed, and send them a priced change order to approve — before you do the extra work. No more unpaid extras.
